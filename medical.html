Why It Matters

Healthcare Communication is Life-Critical

Miscommunication in a medical setting is not just inconvenient — it can have serious consequences for patient safety and outcomes. Deaf and hard-of-hearing patients have a legal right under the ADA, Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, and the Affordable Care Act to effective communication access in all healthcare settings.

A qualified, certified ASL interpreter is the gold standard for effective communication. Rose SLI's medical interpreters are trained in clinical terminology, HIPAA compliance, and the emotional sensitivity required in patient-facing healthcare environments.

Healthcare Settings

Medical Settings We Serve

Primary Care & Specialist Visits

Routine checkups, specialist consultations, pre-op appointments, and post-operative follow-ups. We ensure patients can communicate directly with their providers and fully understand their care plan.

Mental Health & Behavioral Health

Therapy sessions, psychiatric evaluations, substance use counseling, and crisis interventions. Our interpreters approach mental health interpreting with the highest degree of sensitivity and ethical care.

Hospital & Emergency Settings

Emergency departments, inpatient care, surgical support, and ICU consultations. We prioritize urgent and same-day requests and maintain 24/7 VRI availability for emergency situations.

Telehealth & VRI

Remote video interpreting for virtual care platforms including telemedicine visits, remote consultations, and online mental health sessions. We integrate with all major telehealth platforms.

Informed Consent & Patient Education

Ensuring patients fully understand diagnoses, procedures, risks, and aftercare instructions. Effective communication during the consent process is both a legal and ethical obligation.

Dental, Vision & Allied Health

Dental offices, optometry clinics, physical therapy, occupational therapy, audiology appointments, and other allied health settings — all covered with certified ASL interpretation.

Compliance

HIPAA Compliance & Certification

Every Rose SLI medical interpreter is trained in HIPAA Privacy and Security rules and understands their obligations regarding Protected Health Information (PHI). We treat all patient information with strict confidentiality, consistent with the standards expected of your clinical staff.

  • All interpreters RID-certified (NIC, CI/CT, or equivalent)
  • HIPAA Privacy Rule training completed and maintained
  • PHI handled with the same standards as clinical staff
  • Strict impartiality — interpreters do not offer personal medical advice
  • Experience with sensitive topics: mental health, reproductive health, chronic illness, end-of-life
  • Available for on-site and telehealth (VRI) appointments
  • Short-notice and same-day scheduling for urgent appointments
  • Business Associate Agreements (BAA) available upon request
